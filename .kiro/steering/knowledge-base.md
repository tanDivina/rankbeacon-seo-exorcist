## SEO Terminology Dictionary

### Technical SEO Terms
- **Crawl Budget**: Number of pages search engines will crawl on your site
- **Canonical Tag**: Indicates the preferred version of duplicate/similar pages
- **Robots.txt**: File that tells search engines which pages to crawl/ignore
- **XML Sitemap**: List of all important pages for search engine discovery
- **Core Web Vitals**: User experience metrics (LCP, FID, CLS)
- **Index Coverage**: Which pages are included in search engine indexes

### Content SEO Terms
- **Search Intent**: The goal behind a user's search query
- **Long-tail Keywords**: Specific, longer search phrases with lower competition
- **Featured Snippet**: Highlighted answer box at top of search results
- **AI Overview**: AI-generated summary in search results (formerly SGE)
- **Keyword Cannibalization**: Multiple pages competing for same keyword
- **Topic Cluster**: Group of related content linked to pillar page

### Link Building Terms
- **Backlink**: Incoming link from another website
- **Domain Authority**: Metric predicting ranking potential (0-100)
- **Anchor Text**: Clickable text in a hyperlink
- **Nofollow Link**: Link that doesn't pass SEO value
- **Link Velocity**: Rate at which site gains new backlinks
- **Toxic Links**: Low-quality backlinks that can harm rankings

## Frequently Asked Questions

### How long does SEO take to show results?
Typically 3-6 months for noticeable improvements, 6-12 months for significant gains. Factors include:
- Site age and existing authority
- Competition level in your niche
- Quality and consistency of optimization efforts
- Technical foundation and site health
- Content quality and publishing frequency

### What's the most important ranking factor?
There's no single factor, but key priorities include:
1. **Content Quality**: Comprehensive, relevant, valuable content
2. **Backlinks**: Quality and relevance of linking domains
3. **Technical SEO**: Fast, mobile-friendly, crawlable site
4. **User Experience**: Engagement metrics and Core Web Vitals
5. **Relevance**: Matching search intent and using semantic keywords

### How many keywords should I target per page?
Focus on one primary keyword and 2-5 related secondary keywords per page:
- Primary keyword in title, H1, first paragraph, URL
- Secondary keywords in H2s, body content, naturally integrated
- Semantic variations throughout content
- Avoid keyword stuffing (maintain natural density)

### Should I focus on traffic or rankings?
Focus on **qualified traffic** that converts, not just rankings:
- Target keywords with commercial intent for business goals
- Optimize for user experience to improve engagement
- Track conversions and ROI, not just rankings
- Build topical authority across keyword clusters
- Prioritize featured snippets and AI overview inclusion

### How do I recover from a ranking drop?
1. **Identify the cause**: Algorithm update, technical issue, penalty
2. **Analyze the impact**: Which pages/keywords affected
3. **Check competitors**: Did they gain or also lose rankings
4. **Review recent changes**: Site updates, content changes, link profile
5. **Implement fixes**: Address technical issues, improve content quality
6. **Monitor recovery**: Track rankings and traffic over time

### What's the difference between on-page and off-page SEO?
**On-Page SEO** (factors you control on your site):
- Content quality and optimization
- Title tags, meta descriptions, headers
- Internal linking structure
- Page speed and mobile optimization
- Schema markup and technical SEO

**Off-Page SEO** (factors outside your site):
- Backlink profile and link building
- Brand mentions and citations
- Social signals and engagement
- Online reviews and reputation
- External content and guest posting

## Seasonal SEO Considerations

### Algorithm Update Seasons
- **Core Updates**: Typically 2-4 times per year, broad ranking changes
- **Spam Updates**: Periodic crackdowns on manipulative tactics
- **Product Reviews**: Updates targeting review content quality
- **Helpful Content**: Updates rewarding user-first content

### Preparation Strategies
1. **Monitor industry news**: Stay informed about upcoming updates
2. **Maintain quality standards**: Follow best practices consistently
3. **Diversify traffic sources**: Don't rely solely on organic search
4. **Document changes**: Track what you change and when
5. **Build resilience**: Focus on long-term value, not quick wins

## Emergency Response Protocols

### Sudden Traffic Drop (>30%)
1. Check Google Search Console for manual actions or security issues
2. Verify site is accessible and not blocked by robots.txt
3. Check for recent algorithm updates or industry-wide impacts
4. Review recent site changes or deployments
5. Analyze which pages/keywords lost traffic
6. Implement emergency fixes for critical issues

### Penalty Recovery
1. Identify penalty type (manual action vs. algorithmic)
2. Review Google's quality guidelines
3. Fix identified issues (thin content, unnatural links, etc.)
4. Submit reconsideration request if manual action
5. Monitor recovery progress weekly
6. Document lessons learned for future prevention

### Competitor Surge
1. Analyze what changed for competitor (new content, links, technical)
2. Assess if it's sustainable or temporary
3. Identify your competitive advantages
4. Develop counter-strategy focusing on differentiation
5. Accelerate your content and link building efforts
6. Monitor for opportunities to capture their weaknesses

## Success Metrics and KPIs

### Primary Metrics
- **Organic Traffic**: Total visits from search engines
- **Keyword Rankings**: Positions for target keywords
- **Conversion Rate**: % of visitors completing desired actions
- **Revenue/ROI**: Direct business impact from SEO efforts

### Secondary Metrics
- **Click-Through Rate (CTR)**: % of impressions resulting in clicks
- **Bounce Rate**: % of single-page sessions
- **Time on Page**: Average engagement duration
- **Pages per Session**: Depth of site exploration
- **Index Coverage**: % of pages successfully indexed

### Advanced Metrics
- **Visibility Score**: Estimated traffic potential from rankings
- **Featured Snippet Captures**: Number of position zero rankings
- **AI Overview Inclusion**: Presence in AI-generated summaries
- **Voice Search Rankings**: Performance for conversational queries
- **Competitive Share**: Your visibility vs. competitors
